The story

How a CS engineer ended up planning weddings

Divisha Bedi
Event Manager
Event planning Client management Vendor coordination Budgeting On-ground execution Teaching & mentoring AI tools Canva Creative problem-solving

Started in code. Ended up in beautiful chaos.

I have a B.Tech in Computer Science — which sounds like the wrong background until you realise event management and engineering are basically the same job. Hold a complex system in your head. Anticipate what breaks. Fix it before anyone notices. That instinct goes everywhere with me.

After my MBA in Event Management & PR from NAEMD Mumbai, I freelanced across 15+ companies — weddings, Bollywood film festivals, international exhibitions, IPL. Three years of learning how different teams work, how different clients think, and how to read a room fast.

"Every event is a logistics puzzle with a human being at the centre. The job is to solve it without anyone seeing you solve it."

The mascot brief — my favourite project ever.

A couple wanted a custom character for their event. I designed the mascot — the look, the personality, the written voice. Then for weeks leading up to the event, every message to the couple came from the character. Updates, reminders, timelines — all in persona.

By the time they arrived at the venue, the mascot felt like a real part of their story. It became the detail their guests couldn't stop talking about. That project is why I believe the difference between delivering an event and creating a memory is always a creative decision someone was willing to make.

Patient, warm, and good at making things click.

People tend to relax around me — I think it's because I genuinely don't rush. A nervous client who can't visualise the venue. A vendor who needs a brief explained differently. A junior coordinator on their first big event who's spiralling a little. I take the time. I break things down until they land. I've been told more than once that I'm a good teacher, and I think that's one of the better things you can be in this industry.

Learning what's next — honestly.

I use Claude for writing and scripting, Google Veo for video generation, Canva for visual content, and Google Sheets for project tracking. I'm early in the AI curve and upfront about it. But picking up tools fast and actually using them — that's not new for me.
